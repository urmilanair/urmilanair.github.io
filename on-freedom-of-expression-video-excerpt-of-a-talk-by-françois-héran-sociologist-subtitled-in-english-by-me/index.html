On freedom of expression: video excerpt of a talk by François Héran, sociologist (subtitled in English by me)

March 30, 2021


Description

This video that I have subtitled in English is excerpted from a talk in French by François Héran, sociologist and demographer. It focuses on the idea of freedom of expression (Fr. liberté d’expression). The talk was addressed to history and geography teachers in France in the wake of the murder of Samuel Paty. Paty, himself a history and geography teacher, had used caricatures of the Prophet Muhammad in his class and was consequently killed by a young French jihadist of Chechen origin.

Héran gave this talk on October 30, 2020 during his course on migration at the Collège de France. The talk lasts around fifty minutes. This video includes the six-odd minutes of its conclusion.

Héran’s article on the subject was published the same day in the online French journal La Vie des Idées, a publication of the Collège de France.

In March 2021, he published the book titled Lettre aux professeurs sur la liberté d’expression (2021, Paris: La Découverte, 252p.). In the book, he develops the various analytical threads that he presents in the article and the talk.

The title of the book in English would be On Freedom of Expression: A Letter To Teachers.
